African gods
god country history
Olorun Nigeria He is the Zeus or Jehovah of the Yoruba pantheon. He is the god of the heavens and creator of the world. In reality though, he is more of the planner of the world, as he gave the task of creation to his son Obatala.
Oba He is the goddess of rivers, and sometimes marriage. Oba represents not just the literal rivers, but also the rivers and waters of life.
Igweakala He is the popular God that visited every four years as a masquerade during the December festive period.
Ogbunabali The name means “the one who kills at night” is said to be the Igbo God of death, his victims are criminals and those who committed atrocities.
Gu Benin He is the God of war and the patron deity of craftsmen and smiths, he is the son of Lisa and Mawu. According to mythological stories, he was sent to make the earth a nicer place for people to live, and he has not yet finished this task.
Aken Egypt He is the keeper of the boat which ferries souls across Lily Lake into the Field of Reeds in the afterlife. He is only mentioned in the Book of the Dead.
Ba'al He is the God of the storm originally from Phoenicia. The meaning of the name is "Lord" and he was only worshipped in Egypt in the later period of the New Kingdom (1570-1069 BCE) as a major deity in Canaan.
Seth He is the God of violence, chaos, storms and the deserts. He is either depicted as an animal or a human with an animal head.
Umvelinqangi South Africa The name means “he who was in the very beginning”. He is also regarded as the creator of civilization and is the God of thunder.
Amadlozi Also known as the ancestor of the Zulus, he is the one who grants access to the spirit world.
